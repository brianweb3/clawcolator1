What is a Liquidity Provider in Clawcolator?

Liquidity Providers (LPs) in Clawcolator are inventory holders who provide capital for users to trade against. Unlike traditional protocols, LPs in Clawcolator have enhanced control capabilities through a pluggable matcher program.

Key Features of LPs in Percolator/Clawcolator:

  • Trade Selection: LP can reject trades through their matcher program
  • Pluggable Matcher: LP provides a matching program/context that can implement AMM/RFQ/CLOB logic
  • Position Fungibility: Positions opened with LP1 can be closed against LP2 by design
  • GC Protection: LP accounts are never garbage collected (GC)
  • Variation Margin: The engine uses variation margin semantics, making positions fungible across LPs

How to Become an LP:

1. Deposit Funds:

  • Transfer tokens into the vault SPL account
  • Call RiskEngine::deposit(idx, amount, now_slot)

2. Configure Matcher:

  • Implement the MatchingEngine interface
  • Matcher can implement AMM, RFQ, or CLOB logic
  • Matcher can reject trades that don't meet your criteria

3. Risk Management:

  • Engine checks solvency after each trade
  • If post-trade solvency fails, the trade is rejected
  • LP is not forced to accept every trade

Technical Details:

Trading:

Wrapper validates signatures and oracle input, then calls:
RiskEngine::execute_trade(matcher, lp_idx, user_idx, now_slot, oracle_price, size)

Liquidations:

Liquidations close the liquidated account at oracle price (no LP/AMM required). PnL is routed through the engine's waterfall.

Liquidity Fragmentation:

Possible at the execution layer. Maximum fill against an LP is bounded by that LP's inventory/margin. Aggregation/routing across LPs is a wrapper-level feature.
